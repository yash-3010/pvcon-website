## What is a Safety Signal?

A safety signal is information that arises from one or multiple sources, including observations and experiments, which suggests a new potentially causal association, or a new aspect of a known association, between an intervention and an event or set of related events that is judged to be of sufficient likelihood to justify verificatory action.

## Quantitative Signal Detection Methods

### Disproportionality Analysis

Disproportionality analysis is the most widely used quantitative method for signal detection in spontaneous reporting databases. Common metrics include:

- Proportional Reporting Ratio (PRR)
- Reporting Odds Ratio (ROR)
- Information Component (IC)
- Empirical Bayes Geometric Mean (EBGM)

## Regulatory Expectations

Both the EMA and FDA have clear guidance on signal detection frequency and documentation. MAHs are expected to conduct ongoing signal detection activities and document all signals evaluated, including those that did not meet the threshold for further action.
