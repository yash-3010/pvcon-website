## What is a PSUR?

A Periodic Safety Update Report (PSUR) is a pharmacovigilance document intended to provide an evaluation of the risk-benefit balance of a medicinal product at defined time points after its authorisation.

## PSUR vs PBRER: Key Differences

While PSURs and PBRERs serve similar purposes, there are important structural and content differences:

| Aspect | PSUR | PBRER |
|--------|------|-------|
| Scope | EU focused | Global (ICH E2C(R2)) |
| Format | EU template | ICH harmonised template |
| Benefit-risk | Less structured | Integrated benefit-risk section |

## Submission Timelines and DLP

The Data Lock Point (DLP) and submission deadlines are critical. For centrally authorised products, PSURs must be submitted within 70 days of the DLP. For nationally authorised products, timelines may vary by member state.

## Tips for Efficient PSUR Preparation

1. Maintain a running literature search throughout the reporting period
2. Ensure your safety database queries are validated and reproducible
3. Start the benefit-risk section early — it takes the most time
4. Align with your QPPV well before the DLP
