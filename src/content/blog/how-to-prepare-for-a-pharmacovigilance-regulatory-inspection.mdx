## Types of Pharmacovigilance Inspections

Pharmacovigilance inspections can be triggered routinely or for cause. Routine inspections are planned by national competent authorities or the EMA and assess whether MAHs comply with their pharmacovigilance obligations. For-cause inspections are initiated when specific concerns have been identified.

## The 12-Month Preparation Timeline

### 12 Months Before

- Conduct a full internal audit of your PV system
- Review and update your PSMF
- Ensure all SOPs are current and staff are trained

### 6 Months Before

- Run a mock inspection with an external auditor
- Address all critical and major findings from internal audit
- Verify ICSR database completeness and accuracy

### 1 Month Before

- Prepare inspection-ready document binders
- Brief all staff likely to be interviewed
- Confirm QPPV availability throughout inspection period

## Common Inspection Findings

The most frequently cited deficiencies in EU pharmacovigilance inspections include late ICSR submissions, inadequate signal detection processes, and PSMF deficiencies.
