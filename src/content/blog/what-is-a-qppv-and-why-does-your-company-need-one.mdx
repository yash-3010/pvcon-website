## Introduction

The Qualified Person for Pharmacovigilance (QPPV) is one of the most critical roles in any pharmaceutical company operating within the European Union. Established under EU legislation, the QPPV serves as the single point of contact for all pharmacovigilance activities and is personally responsible for the establishment and maintenance of the pharmacovigilance system.

## What Does a QPPV Do?

The QPPV is responsible for a wide range of activities including:

- Overseeing the Pharmacovigilance System Master File (PSMF)
- Ensuring adverse event reporting compliance
- Managing signal detection and evaluation
- Liaising with regulatory authorities
- Ensuring the quality of all safety data submitted

## Why Is the QPPV Role Legally Required?

Under EU Directive 2001/83/EC and EU Regulation 726/2004, every Marketing Authorisation Holder (MAH) must appoint a QPPV who resides and operates within the European Union. Failure to maintain a qualified QPPV can result in suspension of marketing authorizations.
