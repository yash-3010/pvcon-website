## Overview of GVP Modules

Good Pharmacovigilance Practices (GVP) are a set of measures drawn up to facilitate the performance of pharmacovigilance in the European Union. The GVP guidelines cover the core pharmacovigilance processes for medicines authorised in the EU.

## Key Changes in 2025

The European Medicines Agency released updated guidance across several GVP modules in late 2024 and early 2025. The most significant changes affect:

### Module VI — Management and Reporting of Adverse Reactions

Updated timelines and electronic submission requirements for ICSRs have been clarified. Companies must ensure their safety databases are compatible with the latest E2B(R3) format.

### Module IX — Signal Management

Revised expectations for signal detection frequency in smaller MAHs, with clearer guidance on what constitutes a validated signal versus a potential signal.

## Action Points for MAHs

1. Review your current SOPs against the updated GVP text
2. Update your PSMF to reference the new module versions
3. Conduct gap analysis training for your PV team
4. Verify your ICSR submission timelines meet the new requirements
